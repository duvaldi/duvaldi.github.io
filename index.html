Interest in IT

What is my interest in IT?

It is such a rapid evolving field and industry, which keeps it super exciting. I love gadgets and newer and better tech that works better and faster than anything from yesterday and allow us to achieve so much more.

When did my interest in IT start?

My earliest memories of interests in IT was by dissembling any electronic device I could lay my hands on and being fascinated by the looks of capacitors, resistors etc on the circuit boards. This was probably around the ages of 5-7. And then thinking back about how much fun it was using command line (because that’s all there was at that stage) to access games and install programs after building a computer from scratch.

Was there a particular event or person that sparked my interest?

The person in my life that definitely played a big roll in my interest in IT would’ve been my dad. He use to own a computer shop and I played there A LOT while growing up.

My IT experience outline .

I have limited IT experience at this stage. I use to build computers as a kid and then worked in IT for about 6 moths after completing my A+ and N+ certificates.

Why did I choose to come to RMIT?

I chose RMIT because I liked how they have real world connections with the Industry and seems really focus on teaching information that are relevant to everyday knowledge that one would use in a real job. The university also offered me a position to study and is in a great location!

What do I expect to learn during your studies?

The short answer to what I hope to learn during my studies would be AS MUCH AS POSSIBLE! But I’m hoping to learn how to be a good programmer and want to learn about as many different avenues in IT that I can to see what else sparks an interest. Also to see what other useful skills I can pick up to be a better candidate for jobs that I’m going to apply to.
