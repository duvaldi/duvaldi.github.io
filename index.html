Interest in IT

What is my interest in IT?

It is such a rapid evolving field and industry, which keeps it super exciting. I love gadgets and newer technology that works better and faster than anything from yesterday, which allows us to achieve so much more.

When did my interest in IT start?

My earliest memories of interests in IT was by dissembling any electronic device I could lay my hands on and being fascinated by the looks of capacitors, resistors etc on the circuit boards. This was probably around the ages of 5-7. I also remember how much fun it was using command line (because that’s all there was at that stage) to access games and install programs after building a computer from scratch.

Was there a particular event or person that sparked my interest?

The person in my life that definitely played a big roll in my interest in IT would have been my dad. He use to own a computer shop and I played there A LOT while growing up.

My IT experience outline .

I have limited IT experience at this stage. I used to build computers as a kid and then worked in IT for about 6 months after completing my A+ and N+ certificates.

Why did I choose to come to RMIT?

I chose RMIT because I liked how they have real world connections with the industry and seems really focused on teaching information that are relevant to everyday knowledge that one would use in a real job. The university is also in a great location!

What do I expect to learn during my studies?

The short answer to what I hope to learn during my studies would be AS MUCH AS POSSIBLE! But I’m hoping to learn how to be a good programmer and want to learn about as many different avenues in IT to see what else sparks an interest. I would also like to see what other useful skills I can pick up to be a better candidate for jobs that I’m going to apply for.

Personal Profile

  • Myer-Briggs result :

INTJ

What do the results of these tests mean for you?
My results for the Myer-Briggs test shows that my personality is the INTJ type, which stands for Introverted iNtuitive Thinking Judging. Even though I can somewhat relate to a few of the traits (i.e. innovative, independent, value knowledge and have high standards for performance), there are also some I do not relate with. Overall, I am struggling to believe that by filling out around a 100 close-ended questionnaire can put you into 1 of 16 boxes. In addition, I am finding it very difficult to find any concrete scientific evidence that the Myer-Briggs test has any value.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team?
I think I am fairly competent working in most team setups where the team members value taking the task at hand serious and value hard work. It is challenging for me to work with team members with a “don’t care" attitude.

How should you take this into account when forming a team?
I should make sure that the team members have good work ethic, want to achieve good results and do not want to waste time. In addition it would be great to have team members that I get along with and possibly have some sort of a common interest with.
